One of the main design principles for website design is 
simplicity. Jakob Nielsen (1999) originally proposed that 
designers go through all of their design elements and 
remove them one by one. If a design works just as well 
without an element, then remove it. Do you think this is a 
good design principle these days? 
CP650 - Lec 2 25
Simplicity is certainly an important design principle. Many designers try to 
cram too much into a screenful of space, making it unwieldy for people to 
find the information in which they are interested. Removing design elements 
to see what can be discarded without affecting the overall function of the 
website can be a salutary lesson. Unnecessary icons, buttons, boxes, lines, 
graphics, shading, and text can be stripped, leaving a cleaner, crisper, and 
easier-to-navigate website. However, graphics, shading, coloring, branding 
and formatting can make a site aesthetically pleasing and enjoyable to use. 
Good interaction design involves getting the right balance between aesthetic 
appeal and the optimal amount and kind of information.
